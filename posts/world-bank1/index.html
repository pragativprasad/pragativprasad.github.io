Female Employment in South Asia

November 11, 2019 One minute read

I’m particularly interested in development in South Asian countries, so I wanted to see what data World Bank had to offer.

Percent of women in the workforce - out of total workers - in South Asian countries from 1990 to 2018

Strangely, this data shows the percent of women in the workforce reducing since 2000 or the mid-2000s for certain countries.

Percent of self-employed women - out of total employed women - in South Asian countries from 1991 to 2018.

We see this increasing for Sri Lanka and Bhutan, decreasing for India and Maldives, and kind of staying the same for Afghanistan and Nepal.

Social progression and increasing US investment in the Indian technology sector has allowed women to work for big corporations. We see a similar trends in Maldives as the archipelago has become a popular luxury tourist destination.

Comparing the two metrics

This graph is super weird - especially evident for Maldives and Sri Lanka. The % of self-employed women changes so much between each year, eventually seeming like it converges - but this really can’t be the full story.

What’s more likely is that World Bank’s method of measuring social development varies greatly from year to year
